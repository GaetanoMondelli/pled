Digital Collaboration Framework


Once the rules of partecipation are set there is no tool to ensure their application without going through long and sometimes expensive processes.
How many time people do not take a legal action because they cannot afford legal fees or time constraints? How often people do not work together because they cannot define and understand the legal terms?
Distributed ledgers technology (DLT) can track and replicate all the interactions and easily check whether or not they were compliant with the law or with the agreement terms. Today the most known application for DLTs are blockchains and smart contracts which can enforce rules by design but they have some limitations:
  • They require a deep technology knowledge
  • They work only for certain strict scenarios
  • They do not scale well
  • They are resistant to updates
The general approach of DLTs and practical ledgers offers flexibility and allows to take quick decisions. Given a set of rules they can solve conflict or detect misbehaviours or check whether the parties are receiving the deserved benefits. Using one source of truth, Pled discourages people from cheating and promotes collaboration on a larger scale.
